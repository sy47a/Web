Japan

Japan It faces the Pacific Ocean to the east and the East Sea to the west, and is located on the Japanese archipelago extending from near Taiwan Island in East China Sea to the north of the Sea of Okhotsk to the south. The mainland of the Japanese archipelago consists of four large islands: Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku. Geologically, it lies on the Pacific Rim midwifery, also known as the 'ring of fire', and natural disasters such as earthquakes, tsunamis, and volcanic eruptions occur frequently.

Of the four islands, Honshu is larger than the combined area of South Korea and North Korea, and at the same time, it is the seventh largest island in the world. In detail, Kyushu Island, the third island, is slightly larger than Gyeongsang Province, and Shikoku Island, the fourth island, is similar in size to Gyeongsangbuk-do.
